This ranks each pair as a quarterly mean-reversion trade that switches sides each quarter: it goes long whichever name is cheap versus the two stocks' own historical P/E relationship and shorts the other — so it is not a fixed long/short of one name. Disc/prem now is how far the first name's P/E sits versus the second relative to their historical average (negative = first name unusually cheap, the side the trade goes long; positive = unusually rich, so it goes long the second). Hit rate (Q) is the share of quarters the trade made money, Avg / Q the mean quarterly long-short return, Cumulative the compounded result, and IC (Q) the rank correlation between the size of the gap and the next quarter's reversion. Click any pair to expand its quarter-by-quarter record. Green = cheap / made money.

Read with care: the valuation signal needs an 18-month P/E baseline and the uploaded P/E history starts mid-2021, so hit rates rest on only ~14 quarters (end-2022 to mid-2026) — a single regime, and a 70% hit over 13 quarters still carries a wide confidence band. Figures are in-sample, gross of costs, and assume FX-hedged cross-currency legs. Read this as "which pairs reverted reliably in this window," not a forward guarantee.
